3
Set global context · optionali
The global context is a surrounding buffer (≈1.25 km) so streets near the edge are measured against the wider network. Click Set global boundary to generate it — then drag its vertices to adjust if you like. It's optional: if you skip it, Run uses the study area itself as the buffer.
4
Run PWN Analyseri
Compute the Space Syntax measures and destination proximity, then grade the network into the four PWN tiers.
5
Close network gapsi
Find roads that don't score high on their own, but are essential to keep the priority network continuous — short links that bridge two high-value (PWN 1/2) streets. These are promoted as gap closers, an added layer shown distinctly in cyan. Your measured PWN grades are not changed.
Or adjust by hand — pick a brush, then click roads on the map to raise or lower their tier. Useful for stray high-scoring roads that aren't really part of the network, or local roads you know matter.
6
Export resultsi
Export graded street segments by PWN tier. GeoJSON carries full attributes for QGIS; KML is pre-styled by tier colour for Google Earth.

Analysis Radius · walking scale
Radius1250 m
⚠ beyond walking scale
This tool measures the walking network. 1250 m ≈ a 15-min walk and is the intended default — keep it here unless you have a specific reason not to. A larger radius can reveal regional structure over a big study area, but past ~2 km you are measuring movement at vehicular scale, which is a different network than the one people walk.
Space Syntax Composite
A street's Space Syntax score is the strongest of its three measures — Connectivity, Integration, or Choice. A street is a walking priority if it's important for any reason, so the model takes its best quality rather than averaging (averaging flattens differences). Under maxing the three are equal lenses — per-measure weights don't apply.
Weighted average mode: the three measures are blended by the weights below. Lower a measure's weight to reduce its influence — e.g. down-weight Connectivity if you find it too noisy. Weights are relative (they're normalised).
